A Bachelor's degree is needed most of the time from an accredited program for entry-level jobs, with Electrical Engineering or Computer Science degrees also being generally accepted. Master's degree can be earned for advancement and a doctoral degree for research positions.


Becoming a computer engineer can take from four to seven years to complete, but students typically finish computer engineering prerequisites and general education courses needed to land a job in the field.


Hands-on experience in laboratory clinics as well as classroom-based seminars also increase chances of obtaining a higher position.
